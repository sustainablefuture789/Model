That 20 kg from every single person adds up to roughly 650,000 tonnes a year nationally — and it's still growing. Yet Australia has almost no capacity to recover the metals inside those old circuit boards, so they get shipped overseas or buried, and the copper, gold and silver leave with them.

Roughly 78% of the value in Australian e-waste currently leaves the country or goes to landfill.
CHAPTER 02 — THE SOLUTION

We recover it with electricity.

Sarix uses electrochemical recovery — the same proven science behind electroplating, run in reverse. We dissolve the metals out of shredded e-waste, then use a gentle electric current to plate them back out as pure metal. It runs cool, on electricity, with fewer chemicals than smelting — and it works onshore, at modest scale, where almost no one else does.

More current → copper ions cross faster → metal plates thicker on the cathode, while oxygen bubbles off the anode. The copper-sulfate bath needs no added reagent — which is why this step is cheap to run.

CHAPTER 04 — THE COMPOUNDING ASSET

Every discovery becomes a patent.

When deep expertise meets daily lab work, new methods get invented. Sarix's plan is simple: when the team finds a better way to recover a metal, patent it. Each patent does three things at once — it protects Sarix's process, it blocks competitors, and it becomes an asset Sarix can license to others for ongoing royalties.

A patent isn't a cost — it's an asset that earns while you sleep.
